PREDICHESS GUIDE

Comprehensive rules for the tactical prediction system

Core Concept: The Prediction Phase

Predichess is played on a standard chess board with regular pieces, but with a highly crucial twist. Every time you make a move, you do not immediately pass the turn to the opponent. Instead, your screen flips, and you enter the Prediction Mode.

During this mode, you select one of your opponent's pieces and click-and-drag it to designate where you think they will move on their turn. Once submitted, your prediction is locked in silently, and the turn passes to your opponent.

Sprung Traps & Piece Vaporization

When the opponent makes a move, the system automatically checks if it matches your pending prediction:

  • Direct Hit (Trap Sprung!): If they make exactly the move you predicted, a tactical trap is sprung! Their moving piece is vaporized (destroyed) before it can complete its path.
  • Compensation Move: Since their piece was destroyed, they retain the turn to make another move (a compensation move). No predictions are checked on a compensation move, and they cannot trigger traps.
  • Trap Missed: If they make any other move, their move completes normally, and the prediction is cleared without effect.

Tactical Victory Conditions

There are multiple ways to claim victory on the battleground:

  • Checkmate: Traditional chess checkmate wins the game.
  • King Vaporization (The ultimate trap!): If you predict exactly where the opponent will move their King, and they make that move, their King is vaporized! This is an immediate tactical knockout!
  • Resignation: Opponent resigns out of sheer tactical defeat.
